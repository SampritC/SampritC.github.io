About

I am a final-year M.Stat student at the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata, specializing in Theoretical Statistics. My Master’s thesis, supervised by Prof. Bodhisattva Sen (Columbia University), focuses on computational aspects of variational inference and empirical Bayes in high-dimensional linear regression. In parallel, I work with Prof. Nilanjan Chatterjee (Johns Hopkins University) on integrating individual-level and summary-level GWAS data using Random Matrix Theory (RMT) in high-dimensional fixed-asymptotic regimes.

I have been fortunate to pursue research internships /visits at the Department of Biostatistics, Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health (Summer 2024); the Big Data Summer Institute, School of Public Health, University of Michigan (Summer 2023); and the Department of Mathematics and Statistics, IISER Kolkata as an IAS–INSA–NASI Summer Research Fellow (Summer 2022).

Research

My work explores mean-field and structured variational methods, empirical Bayes procedures, and random matrix–based shrinkage for high-dimensional regression and genetic association studies. I am particularly interested in understanding when fast approximate procedures can match or closely track fully Bayesian or likelihood-based benchmarks, both theoretically and empirically.
